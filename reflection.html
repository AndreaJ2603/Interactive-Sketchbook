UI/UX Reflection — Nielsen Heuristics

I think that most heuristics align to Norman’s concerns because it feels that they are all interconected in some ways.

But, Consistency and Standards is more logically aligned to Normans.

I think that these two readings overlap because they both show that experience and mental models are the main factors influencing how users make decisions. In each case users are not analyzing the situation instead they are relying on what they already know or past experience. These past experiences shape the user’s behavior and expectation in how the system should work.

In my project I have identified that User Control and Freedom is the most apparent violation of heuristics principles.

When analyzing my project I noticed that the user does not have sufficent control and freedom when playing the game. I also realized during the designing process that the user during playing the game would not have a choice to go back, stop the game or start over.

The shapes move all in one direction from left to right in the screen. In this case the user has to go through a whole time display of 60 seconds in order to start over.

I think this matters because, as a user, you should have the ability to choose whether you want to continue, go back, or start over without the system stopping you.

Honestly I think that my sketchbook violates most heuristics because it is a project done from scratch and needs a lot of work on it. I did not go online to get sources or ideas and I generated that in my head and tried to implement that with the coding.

But I agree that the major violation I found in my project was consistency and standards. Simply because the actions are not reversible. There is also a lack of consistency, especially when the colors change to red. The speed in which it changes is inconsistent with user expectations, it causes confusion and frustration. As a result, the user has to memorize the timing or be extremely focused to click the mouse at the exact moment the shape turns red.

The Hardest Heuristics to implement

I think that error prevention is the hardest heuristics to implement on a design because errors are a natural part of any design. It’s also important to keep in mind that each user has a different mental state, which can affect how they interact with a system.

I think that it is nearly impossible to prevent or anticipate an error when in a complex system. Adding more constraints of safe guards might create a more complex, confusing and inefficient for the user. Also the concept could get lost in the midst of so much information leading to more error. I think that to design a product to do 100% without any error occurring is impossible because you would have to balance many factors at once.
