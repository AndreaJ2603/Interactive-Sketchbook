Shneiderman's 8 Golden Rules

Which rule feels most absent from your micro-project page?

Permit easy reversal of actions.

I think the reversal action is the most obvious design flaw in my project, and I haven’t been able to resolve it. I considered it during the process, but I couldn’t come up with a solution because I found it too difficult to implement.

Which rule would most improve your sketchbook interface?

Strive for consistency.

I will work on improving consistency on my new upgraded project.

Which rule is hardest to implement honestly?

Prevent errors.

I know that having error is part of design and I will try to make a simpler design to have less possibility of error occurring.

Did any rule contradict how you designed your system originally?
None of these rules have contradicted my design because they all make sense. But I think that when it comes to preventing error may have contradicted my design because I see that error is common even in the best designed system today.
